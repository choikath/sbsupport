Diagnosis of Suspected Ischemic Heart Disease (Stress Testing)
Evidence

Posted: Feb 2016
Team: N. Mull MD

  • Suspected Ischemic Heart Disease
    (or changed clinical status in known IHD pt)
    • Intermediate or High Risk
      Unstable Angina
      (per ACCF/AHA UA/NSTEMI Guidelines)
      • Clinical Assessment: H&P
        If suspected high risk lesions, prior stent in left main coronary artery, or prior sudden death or serious ventricular arrythmia, skip to intiate medical treatment
        • Choose appropriate testing
          or Refer to any technically adequate recent exercise/cardiac imaging study, if avail.

          Otherwise refer to Table below
          • Treatment Recommended

            All: Initiate Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy.

            If high-risk coronary lesion suggested, also consider coronary revascularization to improve survival.

            For others, monitor for improvement after medical therapy. If symptoms don't resolve with medical therapy alone, consider revascularization.
