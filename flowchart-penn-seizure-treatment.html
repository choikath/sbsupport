Treatment of Seizures (Convulsive Status Epilepticus/Non-Convulsive Seizures)

Penn Medicine Formulary

Posted: Jan 2017
Revised: Jan 2019
Team: C. Hill, MD; C. Ellis, MD; M. Caylor, PharmD; J. Levine, MD; Epilepsy and Neurosciences Service Line, Neurocritical Care, Emergency Medicine, Penn Critical Care Committee

  • Treatment of Seizures in Adults
    • Assess seizure:
      Convulsive vs Non-convulsive
      • Convulsive seizures
        Checklist

        1. Safety
         a. Bed rails/pads
         b. Oxygen/suction
         c. Patient on side
        2. Light on, uncover patient, & check camera
        3. Describe aloud what you see
      • Non-convulsive seizures
        Checklist

        1. Safety
         a. Bed rails/pads
         b. Oxygen/suction
         c. Patient on side
        2. Light on, uncover patient, & check camera
        3. Describe aloud what you see
        4. Aura: "what do you feel right now?"
        5. Code word: "Remember purple elephant"
        6. Naming objects
        7. Orientation:
         a. Name?
         b. Location?
         c. Day?
        8. Motor: "Lift your arms"
    • Assess for resolution
      • If seizure stops, conduct post-ictal exam

        1. Code word: "Do you remember the words I gave you?" (non-convulsive only)
        2. "When was your last seizure? What did you feel?"
        3. Naming objects
        4. Orientation:
         a. Name?
         b. Location?
         c. Day?
        5. Motor: "Lift your arms"
      • If seizure persists >2m
        AND patient is not in EMU

        Consider consulting neurology AND treatment guidelines below
        • Convulsive Status Epilepticus
          (Convulsive seizure lasting >5m, OR
          2 convulsive seizures without interim return to baseline mental status)
          • Stabilize the patient

            - Assess airway, breathing, and circulation
            - Give O2 via nasal cannula and assess need for intubation
            - Monitor EKG an blood pressure
            - Note time of seizure onset
            - Check fingerstick: If glucose < 70 mg/dL, administer 25-50 mL of D50 IV
            - Draw labs: CBC, BMP, Mag, toxicology, HCG, and antiepileptic drug levels
            - Neurological exam
              • Treatment
                Time Therapy Medication Special Instructions
                0-10 minFirst-line Therapy If IV Access available:
                Lorazepam IV 4 mg                		 After 5m, consider repeat administration of lorazepam IV 2-4 mg with attention to airway
                If IV Access NOT available:
                midazolam IM 10 mg*
                Divide dose and administer in R/L quadiceps muscles concurrently

                *if patient < 40kg:
                 midazolam IM 5mg                		 After 5m, consider repeat administration of midazolam with attention to airway
                If seizures do not resolve:
                10-30 minSecond-line Therapy Administer:
                levetiracetam IV 3000 mg over 15 min                		May consider alternative:
                Valprioc acid IV 40 mg/kg (over 15 min), OR
                phenytoin IV 20 mg/kg (at 50 mg/min)
                If seizures do not resolve for 20 min after second-line therapy:
                30-60 min
                Intubate AND
                Third-line TherapyLoad with midazolam IV 10 mg &
                Start midazolam infusion at 10 mg/hr                		 For ongoing seizures:
                Repeat midazolam 10mg bolus q10 min and increase infusion rate by 10 mg/hr until seizures cease.

                *May titrate midazolam to a maximum of 2 mg/kg/hr*
                Initiate Continuous EEG, & Notify the ICU
                If seizures resolve:- Discuss further diagnostic workup and management with Neurology Consult
                - Obtain non-contrast head CT
                - If patient's mental status remains altered, initiate continous EEG
                - Continue maintenance levetiracetam 1000 mg q12h (or renally dose adjusted) or alternative second-line medication
                If seizures do not resolve:
                > 60 min
                Continue Evaluation and Treatment- Discuss further management with Neurology Consult and ICU team
                - Obtain non-contrast head CT
                - Contine maintenance levetiracetam 1500 mg q12h (or renally dose adjusted) or alternative second-line medication
                - Consider addition of propofol infusion
                - Add additional antiepileptic drug(s)
                If seizures resolve:- Discuss further diagnostic workup with Neurology Consult
                - Obtain non-contrast head CT
                - Contine maintenance levetiracetam 1500 mg q12h (or renally dose adjusted) or alternative second-line medication
