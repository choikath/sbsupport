  • Pre-Cardiac Cath Anticoag Bridging Pathway (HUP)
    • Guiding Principles
      High bleeding risk procedures (>1.5%) can result in intracranial, intraspinal, intraocular, retropertoneal, intrathoracic, or pericardial bleeding

      Balance risk of bleeding vs Risk of thromboembolic events
      Interval without anticoagulation therapy should be as short as possible.


      *Refer to CHADS2-VASc Score for Afib patients
      • Hold anticoagulant prior to procedure
        Warfarin - 5 d + requires bridging for certain conditions (right)
        DOACs - variable

        Refer to Table below
        • Resume anticoagulants post-procedure

          Warfarin: can resume same day hemostasis achieved.

          Bridging or DOACs should be resumed 12-24h after low-risk procedure (radial access)
          OR 48-72 h for high risk procedure (femoral access)

Conditions for which bridging should be considered
Each patient should be evaluated on a case-by-case basis
  • Mechanical aortic valves with valvular atrial fibrillation
  • Multiple mechanical valves
  • Mechanical mitral valve replacements
  • Venous thromboembolic event within 3mo
  • Prior thrombotic event when off anticoagulants for short duration
  • AF with prior CVA, thromboembolic event, cardiac thrombus or annual CVA risk > 8% (e.g. CHADS2 score >4 OR CHADS2Vasc2 >5
  • Severe thrombophilia
  • Mitral stenosis with atrial fibrillation or previous stroke

- Bridging therapy should begin 2 days AFTER last dose of warfarin was taken
