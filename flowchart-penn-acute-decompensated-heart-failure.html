Posted: Nov 2018
Team: L. Marandola MSN CRNP; K. Gelinne, PA-C; M. Waqar, PA-C; E. King, MD; D. Spatz, MD; N. Mull, MD; J. Wald MD; Y. Quaioit, DNP CRNP; M. O'Malley, MSN RN ACNS-BC; J. Kim, MD; C. Domenico, PharmD; E. Flores, PhD RN;

  • Suspected Acute Decompensated Heart Failure (HUP)
    • Review
      History & Physical
      Order Diagnostic Testing
      • Diagnostic Criteria (Framingham Criteria)
        Requires: >2 Major criteria, OR
        1 Major + 2 Minor criteria

        Refer to Framingham Criteria
        • Assessment
          (NYHA + ACC/AHA Criteria)
          1. HFrEF vs HFpEF
          2. Systolic vs Diastolic
          3. Reason for acute decompensation
          4. NYHA functional class AND ACC/AHA heart failure stage
          • Treatment
            Initiate BOTH pharmacological and non-pharm therapy
            • Pharmacologic therapy

              Diuretic, Beta blocker, ACEi/ARB, Vasodilators, +/- IV iron
              • Non-Pharmacologic therapy

                Nutrition, PT, Telemetry, Education, Sleep med, D/c planning
              Pharmacologic Management (Dosing tables: Initial Daily/Max)
              Agent If active medication If not currently on this medication
              Diuretic
              Furosemide (Lasix)              		 Start IV, 1-1.5 equiv of PO dose Start 40mg IV; q12 hrs
              Beta blocker
              Metoprolol, carvedilol, OR bisoprolol              		 Continue existing BB dose unless one of the following:
              - HR < 50
              - 2nd/3rd AV block
              - Shock              		 Hold BB until clinically and hemodynamically stable
              ACEi/ARB
              (ACEi) Lisinopril, enalapril
              (ARB) Valsartan, losartan              		 Continue unless contraindicated Start ACEi/ARB at low dose unless creatinine >2.5 and K >5.0, and titrate up as tolerated
              Vasodilators
              Hydralazine / Isosorbide mononitrate (Imdur)              		 Continue unless contraindicated*

              *Hydralazine contraindicated in myocardial infarction as increases myocardial oxygen demand              		 Start if ACEi/ARB contraindicated or after BB/ACEi/ARB are uptitrated, and no contraindications*

              *Evidence supports added benefit in African-American patients
              +/- IV Iron supplementation Consider if patient is iron deficient AND if no evidence of infiltrative cardiac disease, myocarditis, or infection
              Dose recommendation: 100mg x 3d

              Go to: Heart Failure Medication Table - ACC

  • EKG and CXR for all patients
  • Tranthoracic echo deferred until euvolemic unless:
    • Patient has new heart failure
    • Patient with existing heart failure if:
      • Significant change in clinical status and >6mo since last echo
      • Recent cardiac event/procedure
      • Received treatment that may recover cardiac function
      • may require ICD


    *If patient has existing ICD, determine what kind of ICD is in place and consult Electrophysiology or call Device Representative for device interrogation. EP consult also appropriate for new or uncontrolled atrial fibrillation.

    Framingham Criteria
    Major Criteria Minor Criteria
  • Acute pulmonary edema
  • Cardiomegaly
  • Hepatojugular reflux
  • Neck vein distention
  • Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea/orthopnea
  • Pulmonary rales
  • S3 gallop
  • Weight loss >4.5 kg in 5 ays in response to treatment
    		•
  • Ankle edema
  • Dyspnea on exertion
  • Hepatomegaly
  • Nocturnal cough
  • Pleural effusion
  • Tachycardia (HR >120)

    NYHA Classification of Heart Failure
    Class Patient Symptoms
    INo limitation of physical activity. Ordinary physical activity does not cause undue fatigue, palpitation, dyspnea (shortness of breath).
    IISlight limitation of physical activity. Comfortable at rest. Ordinary physical activity results in fatigue, palpitation, dyspnea (shortness of breath).
    IIIMarked limitation of physical activity. Comfortable at rest. Less than ordinary activity causes fatigue, palpitation, or dyspnea.
    IV Unable to carry on any physical activity without discomfort. Symptoms of heart failure at rest. If any physical activity is undertaken, discomfort increases.
    Class Objective Assessment
    ANo objective evidence of cardiovascular disease. No symptoms and no limitation in ordinary physical activity.
    BObjective evidence of minimal cardiovascular disease. Mild symptoms and slight limitation during ordinary activity. Comfortable at rest.
    CObjective evidence of moderately severe cardiovascular disease. Marked limitation in activity due to symptoms, even during less-than-ordinary activity. Comfortable only at rest.
    DObjective evidence of severe cardiovascular disease. Severe limitations. Experiences symptoms even while at rest.

    2. Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea
    3. orthopnea
    4. cough (recumbent, ith exertion, nocturnal)
    5. abdominal discomfort/bloating
    6. lower extremity edema, dependent edema
    7.leg heaviness or skin tightness
    8. chest tightness or discomfort
    9. fatigue
    10. syncope/pre-syncope
    11.decreased urine output
    Recent illness Sick contacts, procedures, hospitalizations
    Baseline activity statuss Recent changes? (i.e. winded going up stairs, further distances)
    Baseline oxygen requirement Currently on O2? Uses BiPAP or CPAP?
    Weight Dry weight and any recent weight changes
    Diet Dietary habits including salt and fluid intake
    Etiology of heart failure Per outpatient records if attainable
    Recent studies Echo, cardiac catheterization, or stress test and where study was done
    Comorbidities and Family History HTN, MI, HLD, CAD, valvular disease, thyroid disease, DM, OSA, renal failure, anemia
    Surgical / devices Cardiac, valve repair/replacement, stents, transplant
    Presence of ICD and why it was placed
    Social Tobacco use, ETOH use, drug use (specifically cocaine); psychosocial barriers including access to medicine, home scale, transportation, insurance, etc
    Medications Specifically: diuretics, BB, ACEi/ARB, and other heart failure meds
    Mediation compliance, and any recent medication changes
    Cardiologist? Ourpatient cardiologist and date of late appointment
